III-weak

Verbs with a weak third radical are formed on the basis of the strong verb, while following three rules:

  • if radical three is word-final, it disappears (*ibni’ > ibni)
  • if radical three is followed by a consonant, the preceding vowel is lengthened (*bani’tum > banītum)
  • if radical three is followed by a vowel, vowel contraction occurs (*ibni’ū > ibnû)

I-weak

Verbs with a weak first radical are of several different types, which are listed below.

(1) Verbs I-a are formed on the basis of the strong verb, while following four rules:

  • if radical one is word-initial, it disappears (*’akālum > akālum)
  • if radical one is followed by a consonant, the preceding vowel is lengthened (*i’kul > īkul)
  • if radical one is between two vowels, it and the vowel after it disappear (*i’akkal > ikkal)
  • in the G-stems, infinitives and imperatives start with a- (G-imperative: akul, not *ukul)

(2) alākum is a I-a verb which is irregular in the G-stems:

  • if radical one is followed by a consonant, it assimilates to that consonant (*i’lik > illik, not *īlik)
  • its vowel class is a/i (durative: illak, preterite: illik)

(3) Verbs I-e are formed the like the verbs I-a, exept that they have an e where the verbs I-a have an a.

(4) Action verbs I-w are formed according to the table below.

(5) State-verbs I-w are conjugated like verbs I-a in the finite forms (durative, preterite, perfect) of the G-stem, but otherwise they’re the same as the other I-w verbs.

(6) Verbs I-n are formed on the basis of the strong verb, while following their own set of rules:

  • if radical one is followed by a consonant, it assimilates to that consonant (*indin > iddin)
  • in the imperative, initial n- disappears (G-imperative: idin, not *nidin)
