Laozi, the Old Master, teaches us:
‘Those who know don’t talk, and those who talk don’t know.’

And I hear them talk, they talk and talk and talk:
‘That’s how the world works,’ they say.

The whole world, confidently boiled down to one thing:
It’s all money one day, all power the next.

Easy to understand, it’s all politics and selfishness,
Never just people living lives and dreaming dreams.

If only they’d stop talking and look around them!
But why look at the world if you already know what it’s like?



Solzhenitsyn teaches us:
‘The line dividing good and evil cuts through the heart of every human being.’

They point to somewhere and shout angrily,
‘They are the ones who should do something about it!’

‘I’m just a grain of sand in the desert,’
Says the whole desert choir.

If you want to fight evil, look for it in yourself.
And don’t tell me it’s meaningless, you tiny grain of sand,

For how does evil come into this world
If not through people who don’t fight their own heart?



Jesus teaches us to love all people.
‘Oh, how naive!’ they laugh.

But tell me why you would do anything good for the world
If you hate the terrorists and rapists who inhabit it.

And don’t give such shallow definitions of ‘good’ as ‘pleasing’ or ‘comfortable’;
Does a mother not punish her child because she loves him?

You must want to shift the line in the hearts of the tyrants and the murderers;
What good is it to lock them up and throw away the key?

If everyone who strays should fall into the pits you’ve dug,
Soon the holy path would be empty.



They talk and they talk and they talk,
And I hear the talking voices in my own head, too.

These unhelpful thoughts, always consious, I call ‘rocks’;
Intuition, always unconsious, I call ‘the cicadas’.

Rocks tumble into your head and tell you they know;
They try to persuade you to do something for them.

The cicadas’ steady chirping is easily drowned out by the talking of the rocks,
But following their loving guidance impossible to regret.

Empty your head of rocks and listen to the cicadas:
This is what I teach myself.
